Apex Systems Compensation Guide
Effective January 1, 2026

This guide explains how Apex Systems structures base pay, variable pay, equity, and benefits. It is distributed to every employee at hire and republished annually after the September compensation review.

Compensation philosophy
Apex targets the 65th percentile of base salary for our peer group of venture-backed enterprise software companies in the United States. Total target compensation, including variable pay and equity, is positioned at the 75th percentile. Pay is benchmarked twice per year using Radford survey data and adjusted for geographic cost of labor.

Career levels and pay bands
Apex operates a single level framework across all functions, with seven levels: L1 Associate, L2 Specialist, L3 Senior, L4 Staff, L5 Principal, L6 Director, and L7 Vice President. Each level has a published base salary band with a minimum, midpoint, and maximum. The midpoint represents the target for a fully proficient employee at that level. Employees newly promoted into a level typically enter between the minimum and the midpoint.

Geographic differentials
Base salary is adjusted based on the employee's primary work location. Tier 1 locations include San Francisco Bay Area, New York City, and Seattle; salaries are set at 100% of the published band. Tier 2 includes Boston, Washington DC, Los Angeles, and Chicago at 95%. Tier 3 covers all other U.S. locations at 90%.

Variable pay
Non-sales roles are eligible for an annual performance bonus of 10% of base salary at on-target performance. Payouts range from 0% to 200% of target based on a combination of company performance against the annual plan and individual rating from the September review. Bonuses are paid in March of the following year.

Sales compensation
Sales roles operate on a separate plan with a 50/50 or 60/40 base-to-variable split depending on quota-carrying status. Commissions are paid monthly against booked, signed, and invoiced revenue. Accelerators apply above 100% of quota attainment, and decelerators apply below 50%.

Equity
All employees receive stock options at hire, granted at the fair market value established by the most recent 409A valuation. Grants vest over four years with a one-year cliff and monthly vesting thereafter. Refresh grants are awarded each September based on performance and tenure, targeting approximately 25% of the original new-hire grant annually.

Merit increases and promotions
Base salary increases occur in the September cycle. Typical annual merit increases range from 3% to 5% for employees rated "meets expectations" and up to 10% for those rated "exceeds" or "exceptional." Promotions carry a separate market adjustment that positions the employee at or above the minimum of the new band.

Benefits
Apex covers 100% of the employee premium and 70% of the dependent premium for a Blue Cross PPO medical plan, a Delta Dental PPO plan, and a VSP vision plan. Life insurance of 2x base salary and short-term and long-term disability are provided at no cost. The 401(k) plan offers a 4% company match, vesting immediately. Additional benefits include a monthly wellness stipend of $75, a commuter benefit of up to $300 per month, and fully funded access to the Carrot fertility and family-planning platform.

Payroll
Employees are paid semi-monthly, on the 15th and the last business day of each month. Paystubs are available in the ADP portal. Questions about payroll, deductions, or tax forms should be directed to the payroll team through the HR portal.

Confidentiality
Compensation information is confidential. Discussion of individual compensation outside of HR, legal, and an employee's direct chain of management is discouraged; retaliation for such discussion is prohibited under federal law.
