Apex Systems Leave Policy
Effective January 1, 2026

This document defines all categories of paid and unpaid leave available to Apex Systems employees. It applies to all regular full-time employees based in the United States. Employees working outside the United States should consult the local addendum maintained by the HR Business Partner for their region, which may provide different or additional entitlements required by local law.

Paid Time Off

Full-time employees accrue PTO at the rate of 1.67 days per calendar month, for a total of 20 days per year. Accrual begins on the first day of employment and is credited at the start of each calendar month. Part-time employees working at least 20 hours per week accrue PTO on a prorated basis.

PTO encompasses vacation, personal days, and any other discretionary time away from work that does not fall into one of the specific categories below. Employees are encouraged to use their PTO throughout the year to maintain work-life balance. Managers are responsible for proactively planning coverage to ensure employees can take leave without jeopardizing customer commitments.

PTO may be taken in minimum increments of half a day. Requests of less than three consecutive days require at least three business days of advance notice. Requests of three or more consecutive days require manager approval submitted through the HR portal at least two weeks before the intended start date.

Up to five unused PTO days may be carried over into the following calendar year. Any accrual above the five-day carryover cap is forfeited on December 31. Employees who were unable to use PTO due to business-critical needs may apply to their HR Business Partner for an exception carryover of up to five additional days, subject to VP approval.

Employees leaving the company are paid out for all accrued but unused PTO in their final paycheck, subject to the laws of the employee's home state. California employees are paid out at 100% regardless of any forfeiture provisions above; other states vary.

Sick Leave

Sick leave is provided separately from PTO at 10 days per year, front-loaded on January 1. New hires receive a prorated sick leave balance on their start date based on the number of months remaining in the calendar year. Sick leave may be used for: personal illness or injury, medical or dental appointments, mental health days, preventive care, and care for an immediate family member who is ill, injured, or attending a medical appointment.

Absences of three consecutive days or more require a note from a licensed healthcare provider confirming the employee's inability to work and the anticipated return date. The note does not need to include a diagnosis. Apex does not require medical notes for absences of one or two days.

Unused sick leave does not carry over from year to year and is not paid out on separation. In states that mandate sick leave accrual rather than front-loading, the policy is administered in compliance with the applicable state law.

Parental Leave

Apex provides 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave for the primary caregiver and 8 weeks of fully paid parental leave for the secondary caregiver. These benefits apply to the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child and to both biological and adoptive parents.

Leave may be taken in a single continuous block or split into a maximum of two separate blocks within 12 months of the qualifying event. Employees must notify their manager and HR at least 30 days before the intended start date when the timing is foreseeable. For birth events, notification at the time of awareness is expected; the 30-day requirement is waived if the birth occurs earlier than anticipated.

During parental leave, Apex continues all benefits at the normal employee premium contribution rate. Parental leave runs concurrently with any federal Family and Medical Leave Act entitlements. Employees who have worked at Apex for fewer than 12 months or fewer than 1,250 hours in the preceding 12 months are not eligible for FMLA protection but remain eligible for paid parental leave.

The returning employee has the right to the same position or an equivalent position with the same base pay, bonus opportunity, and equity. Apex does not permit role changes, team transfers, or reporting structure changes initiated by the company during an employee's parental leave without the employee's written consent.

Managers may not contact employees on parental leave for work-related matters except in documented emergencies with HR Business Partner approval. Employees who voluntarily maintain contact with the team during leave do so at their own discretion.

Bereavement Leave

Employees receive up to 5 paid days of bereavement leave for the loss of a spouse, domestic partner, child, parent, sibling, or grandparent. Employees receive up to 3 paid days for the loss of an in-law, step-parent, step-sibling, or step-child. Employees receive up to 1 paid day for the loss of a close friend or colleague at the manager's discretion.

Bereavement leave may be taken non-consecutively within 30 days of the death to accommodate travel, services, or estate obligations. Proof of death, such as an obituary or death certificate, may be requested for absences of more than three days.

Additional unpaid leave beyond these limits may be requested through the HR Business Partner and is evaluated case by case with consideration for the employee's circumstances, team capacity, and business needs.

Jury Duty and Civic Obligations

Apex pays full base salary for employees called to jury duty or subpoenaed as a witness in a legal proceeding unrelated to Apex business. There is no cap on the number of days covered. The employee must submit a copy of the court summons to the payroll team through the HR portal before the first day of service. Any payment received from the court for jury service may be retained by the employee.

Employees are expected to report to work on days when they are released from jury duty before 15:00 local time. On days they are dismissed before noon, they are expected to work for the remainder of the afternoon if feasible.

Military leave is provided in accordance with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act. Employees returning from qualifying military service are reinstated to the same or an equivalent position with all seniority, pay, and benefits restored as if the employee had not been absent.

Sabbatical

After five continuous years of full-time service, employees are eligible for a four-week paid sabbatical. The sabbatical is intended for personal renewal, exploration, or rest and is not intended for secondary employment, competitive activities, or consulting. After ten years of continuous service, employees become eligible for an eight-week paid sabbatical.

Sabbatical requests must be submitted to the employee's manager and HR Business Partner at least 90 days before the intended start date. The department head and HR Business Partner jointly approve or defer the request. Deferral of more than 180 days requires CFO approval and compensation of one week of additional PTO per 90-day deferral period.

Sabbaticals must be taken as a continuous block and may not be split. Sabbatical time does not accrue PTO. Benefits continue during sabbatical at no additional cost to the employee. Unused sabbatical eligibility is forfeited on separation and is not paid out.

Company Holidays

Apex observes 11 paid holidays each calendar year. Employees working in the United States receive:

New Year's Day (January 1), Martin Luther King Jr. Day (third Monday in January), Presidents' Day (third Monday in February), Memorial Day (last Monday in May), Juneteenth (June 19), Independence Day (July 4), Labor Day (first Monday in September), Thanksgiving Day (fourth Thursday in November), the day after Thanksgiving (Friday), Christmas Eve (December 24), and Christmas Day (December 25).

When a holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the preceding Friday. When it falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the following Monday. Employees required to work on a company holiday because of customer coverage, on-call rotation, or operational necessity receive a compensatory day off to be taken within the same quarter.

Employees in jurisdictions with additional statutory holidays receive those days in addition to the 11 company holidays listed above. A schedule of regional holiday additions is published annually in the HR portal.

Leave Request Procedure

PTO and sick leave are requested through the HR portal under the Time Off section. Immediate managers are notified automatically upon submission. For planned PTO requests longer than 5 consecutive days, the manager has 5 business days to approve or request a schedule change; requests are not automatically denied.

Parental leave, sabbatical, bereavement leave, medical leave, and military leave are initiated by contacting the HR Business Partner directly, who provides the required forms and coordinates the leave calendar. All leave requests involving a health condition are handled under strict confidentiality by HR; managers receive only the start date, expected return date, and whether the leave is approved.

Employees should mark their Apex calendar and Slack status for any absence longer than one business day so that colleagues have visibility for scheduling purposes.

Unpaid Leave

Unpaid personal leave of up to 30 days may be granted at manager discretion after all accrued paid leave has been exhausted. Requests for unpaid leave must be submitted to the HR Business Partner with a brief explanation of the need. Benefits, including medical, dental, and vision, continue during approved unpaid leave, with the employee responsible for the standard employee-share premium, which may be billed directly during the leave period. Equity vesting continues during unpaid leave of up to 30 days; longer unpaid leaves may pause vesting.

Medical leave under the FMLA or state family leave laws follows applicable statutory rules for continuation of benefits and job protection. Employees should contact their HR Business Partner as early as possible when a medical leave is anticipated.

Questions about leave eligibility, accrual balances, or scheduling conflicts should be directed to the HR Business Partner for the employee's department. Accrual balances are also visible at any time in the ADP Self-Service portal.
