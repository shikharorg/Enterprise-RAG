Apex Systems Code of Conduct
Effective January 1, 2026

This Code of Conduct sets out the standards of professional behavior expected of every Apex Systems employee, contractor, and temporary worker. It applies to all work-related activities regardless of location, including in-office work, remote work, travel, off-site events, and interactions conducted through digital channels. Apex Systems expects everyone to read this document, understand its contents, and uphold its standards in every professional interaction.

Our Commitment to a Respectful Workplace

Apex Systems is committed to maintaining a work environment that is professional, inclusive, and free from harassment, discrimination, and intimidation. Every person who works at or with Apex Systems deserves to be treated with dignity and respect. This commitment extends to relationships with colleagues, direct reports, managers, customers, vendors, partners, and anyone else encountered in a professional capacity.

Professionalism and Workplace Behavior

Employees are expected to conduct themselves with integrity and professionalism at all times. This includes arriving prepared and on time to meetings, communicating clearly and respectfully in writing and in person, honoring commitments to teammates and stakeholders, and maintaining confidentiality about sensitive business and personnel information.

Disagreements, debates, and competing ideas are a normal and valued part of work at Apex Systems. Employees are encouraged to challenge ideas constructively, offer alternative perspectives, and advocate for their positions. However, disagreement must remain focused on ideas and work products, not on individuals. Personal attacks, condescension, dismissiveness, or hostile communication are not acceptable in any context.

Employees should avoid language or behavior in the workplace that a reasonable person would find offensive, demeaning, or exclusionary. This includes offensive jokes, slurs, stereotyping, deliberate use of incorrect pronouns, and comments about physical appearance, age, religion, national origin, or other personal characteristics. Such behavior will be treated as a conduct violation regardless of the intent behind it.

Anti-Harassment Policy

Apex Systems prohibits harassment of any kind in the workplace. Harassment is unwanted conduct that is severe or pervasive enough to create a hostile, abusive, or offensive work environment, or that results in an adverse employment decision. Harassment may be verbal, written, physical, or visual in nature.

Sexual harassment is a specific and serious form of prohibited conduct. It includes, but is not limited to: unwanted sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexually explicit or suggestive comments, sharing sexually explicit images or materials in the workplace, and any conduct of a sexual nature that is unwanted by the recipient. Sexual harassment by a person in a position of authority over another employee is treated with particular seriousness given the power imbalance involved.

Other forms of prohibited harassment include conduct targeting an individual based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law. Harassment by customers, vendors, or third parties toward Apex Systems employees is also covered by this policy. Employees who are harassed by an external party should report the conduct to their manager or People Operations.

A single isolated incident may constitute harassment if it is sufficiently severe. A pattern of less severe incidents that together create a hostile environment also constitutes harassment. Employees do not need to explicitly object to conduct for it to be considered harassment.

Anti-Discrimination Policy

Employment decisions at Apex Systems are made on the basis of merit, qualifications, and business need. No employment decision — including hiring, promotion, compensation, assignment, discipline, or termination — will be made on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, pregnancy, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

Employees who believe they have been subjected to discriminatory treatment in an employment decision should report the concern to People Operations. Apex Systems will investigate all such reports promptly and take corrective action where warranted.

Conflicts of Interest

A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal interests, financial interests, or outside relationships could influence or appear to influence their judgment or actions on behalf of Apex Systems. Employees are expected to make decisions solely in the best interest of Apex Systems and must disclose any situation that could create a conflict.

Common examples of conflicts of interest include: having a financial stake in a vendor, customer, or competitor; hiring or supervising a close family member or romantic partner; accepting gifts or entertainment from vendors or business partners beyond the limits described in this policy; working for or consulting with a competitor, vendor, or customer while employed at Apex Systems; and using Apex Systems resources, information, or relationships for personal gain.

The company does not prohibit all outside activities. However, employees must disclose any outside employment, board membership, advisory role, or financial interest that could reasonably create or appear to create a conflict of interest. Disclosures are submitted to People Operations and reviewed by the Chief People Officer and General Counsel. The company may require an employee to modify the outside activity or disengage from it if a material conflict is found.

Gift and Entertainment Policy

Employees may not accept or provide gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel with a value exceeding one hundred fifty dollars from or to any vendor, customer, or business partner. Gifts of cash or cash equivalents including gift cards are never permitted regardless of amount. Gifts must not be accepted or given in connection with a pending or anticipated business decision. Employees who receive unsolicited gifts that exceed the limit must return them or surrender them to People Operations.

Entertainment including meals and events is permitted at a reasonable level when there is a legitimate business purpose and the cost is not lavish. Tickets to sporting events, concerts, or similar events may be accepted if the vendor or customer is present. Entertainment that occurs in a context that could embarrass Apex Systems or compromise a business relationship should be declined.

Employees should disclose any gift or entertainment above fifty dollars in the conflict of interest disclosure process.

Confidentiality and Protection of Company Information

Employees have access to confidential and proprietary information in the course of their employment. This includes financial data, customer lists and contracts, product roadmaps, source code, trade secrets, employee compensation data, strategic plans, and any other information that Apex Systems has not made publicly available.

Employees must not disclose confidential information to anyone outside the company without proper authorization, and must not use confidential information for personal benefit. The obligation to protect confidential information extends beyond the period of employment. Upon departure from Apex Systems, employees must return all company property and may not retain, copy, or use any confidential information for any purpose.

Social media use that discloses confidential business information, disparages Apex Systems, customers, or colleagues, or creates legal risk for the company is prohibited. Employees may share general positive statements about working at Apex Systems on social media but should not discuss non-public business developments, customers, or specific products without prior approval from the Communications team.

Use of Company Assets

Company property including computers, phones, software, networks, and physical office resources are provided for business purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided it does not interfere with work performance, involve inappropriate content, or create a security risk. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company devices or networks.

Employees may not use company assets to operate a personal business, to access or distribute illegal content, to circumvent security controls, or to engage in any activity prohibited by this Code of Conduct or applicable law. Misuse of company assets is a disciplinary matter.

Reporting Concerns

Apex Systems expects employees to report conduct that violates this Code of Conduct, company policy, or applicable law. Reporting a concern in good faith is protected conduct. Apex Systems will not retaliate against any employee who reports a concern, participates in an investigation, or requests an accommodation under this policy.

Concerns may be reported to an employee's direct manager, the manager's manager, People Operations, or the General Counsel. For concerns that involve a manager, employees are encouraged to go directly to People Operations or the General Counsel. An anonymous reporting hotline is also available; details are posted on the company intranet.

Reports of harassment, discrimination, conflicts of interest, financial misconduct, security incidents, or potential legal violations should be made promptly. Delay in reporting may complicate investigation and corrective action.

Investigation Process

All reports of Code of Conduct violations are taken seriously and investigated promptly. People Operations leads investigations of workplace conduct violations with involvement from the General Counsel as appropriate. Investigations of financial or legal matters are led by the General Counsel with involvement from the CFO.

The investigator will interview the reporting employee, the subject of the complaint, and any witnesses. All participants in the investigation are expected to cooperate fully and maintain confidentiality to the extent possible. Interference with an investigation, including attempting to discourage witnesses from cooperating or sharing false information, is a serious violation of this policy.

Upon completion of the investigation, the company will take appropriate corrective action where a violation is substantiated. Both the reporting employee and the subject of the complaint will be informed of the outcome, to the extent permitted by confidentiality obligations. The timeline for investigations varies depending on complexity, but Apex Systems aims to conclude investigations within thirty days of the initial report.

Disciplinary Process

Apex Systems uses a progressive discipline framework for most conduct violations, though the company reserves the right to skip steps or proceed directly to termination depending on the severity of the violation.

Verbal Warning: A verbal warning is the first step in the disciplinary process for less serious or first-time violations. The manager delivers the verbal warning in a private meeting, describes the specific conduct at issue, explains the expectation going forward, and documents the conversation in writing, which is filed with People Operations. The employee has an opportunity to respond.

Written Warning: If the behavior is not corrected following a verbal warning, or if the initial violation is of moderate severity, a written warning is issued. The written warning describes the prior verbal warning if applicable, the specific conduct in question, the performance or behavioral standard that was violated, and the consequences of continued non-compliance. The written warning is signed by the manager, the employee, and People Operations and placed in the employee's personnel file.

Performance Improvement Plan: For issues involving sustained performance or behavioral deficiencies, a Performance Improvement Plan may be issued in lieu of or in addition to a written warning. The PIP defines specific, measurable improvement goals, the timeline for achieving them (typically thirty to ninety days), the support the company will provide, and the consequences of failure to meet the plan goals. Progress against the PIP is reviewed at regular intervals during the plan period.

Suspension: In cases where the company needs to conduct an investigation or where a serious violation has occurred that may or may not warrant termination, an employee may be placed on paid administrative leave pending the outcome of the investigation.

Termination: Termination is appropriate when prior disciplinary steps have not resulted in improvement, when the violation is too severe to address through progressive discipline, when the employee has engaged in dishonesty, harassment, violence or threats of violence, theft, serious security violations, or conduct that puts the company or its customers at risk, or when the employment relationship is otherwise untenable. Involuntary terminations are approved by the employee's manager, People Operations, and the department head. Terminations of director-level and above employees additionally require approval from the CEO.

Mandatory Reporting and Zero Tolerance

Certain conduct violations are treated as zero-tolerance matters that result in immediate termination without prior progressive discipline. These include: physical violence or threats of violence, sexual assault or sexual coercion, theft or embezzlement of company funds, willful destruction of company property, falsification of financial records, disclosure of material non-public financial information, and possession of weapons on company premises.

Managers who witness or receive a report of a zero-tolerance violation must immediately escalate to People Operations and the General Counsel. No manager may unilaterally decide not to escalate a potential zero-tolerance violation.

Acknowledgment and Annual Training

All employees are required to read and acknowledge this Code of Conduct upon hire and annually thereafter. Acknowledgment is recorded in the HRIS. Annual training on the key provisions of this Code, including the anti-harassment policy, conflict of interest policy, and reporting procedures, is required for all employees and must be completed within thirty days of the annual rollout. New hires must complete the training within their first fourteen days.

Managers have an elevated responsibility under this Code. In addition to the obligations above, managers must create and maintain an environment in which employees feel safe reporting concerns, must not engage in or permit retaliatory conduct, and must escalate reports of potential misconduct promptly. Failure by a manager to uphold these elevated obligations is itself a disciplinary matter.
