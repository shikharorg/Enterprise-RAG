Apex Systems Expense and Travel Guidelines
Effective January 1, 2026

These guidelines govern how Apex Systems employees incur and report reimbursable business expenses. They apply to all employees, contractors with an expense profile, and board members traveling on Apex business.

Expense management system
All expenses must be submitted through the Navan platform within 30 days of the transaction date. Expenses submitted more than 60 days after the transaction date are not reimbursable except in documented exceptional circumstances approved by the finance business partner. Receipts are required for any individual expense of $25 or more; itemized receipts are required for meals.

Reimbursable categories
The following categories are reimbursable when incurred for legitimate Apex business:
  - Travel: airfare, ground transportation, lodging, and related fees.
  - Meals: while traveling, while hosting customers or candidates, or during required off-site work.
  - Customer entertainment: meals, events, and gifts that comply with customer anti-bribery policies.
  - Software and subscriptions: tools required for the employee's work and not already provided centrally, subject to prior IT approval.
  - Professional development: registration fees, books, and courses within the annual learning budget.
  - Home office: one-time stipend of $1,000 for new remote hires and a quarterly stipend of $150 for ongoing remote employees.

Air travel
Economy class is the standard for flights of six hours or less in total flight time. Premium economy is permitted for flights between six and nine hours, and business class is permitted for flights greater than nine hours or for overnight flights immediately preceding a work commitment. First class is never reimbursed. Flights must be booked at least 14 days in advance except in documented urgent circumstances; advance-purchase discounts are taken whenever available.

Lodging
Hotel rates are capped at the per-city ceiling published in the Navan travel profile. For cities without a published ceiling, the cap is $350 per night including taxes. Room service, in-room movies, mini-bar, and spa services are not reimbursable.

Meals and per diem
When traveling, employees may choose between actual-cost reimbursement and the daily per diem of $75 for U.S. destinations and $100 for international destinations. Receipts are not required for per diem. Employees hosting a customer or candidate meal may expense up to $100 per attendee; a list of attendees and their employer must accompany the submission.

Ground transportation
Rideshare, taxis, shuttles, and standard rental cars are reimbursable. Rental cars are limited to the intermediate class; luxury and sport categories require advance approval. Personal vehicle mileage is reimbursed at the current IRS rate published annually in the Navan profile.

Alcohol
Reasonable alcoholic beverages consumed as part of a business meal are reimbursable when local law permits. Alcohol charged outside a meal context is not reimbursable.

Non-reimbursable expenses
Personal items, traffic fines, airline lounge memberships, airline status-chasing itineraries, home internet upgrades, personal entertainment subscriptions, and any expenses incurred while on personal travel are not reimbursable. Expenses that combine personal and business purposes must be itemized; only the business portion is eligible.

Corporate card
Employees in roles that require frequent travel receive an Apex corporate card issued through Navan. Cardholders are responsible for submitting receipts within 7 days of each transaction. Missing or late submissions may result in suspension of card privileges. The corporate card must not be used for personal purchases.

Exceptions and disputes
Exception requests must be submitted in Navan before the expense is incurred whenever possible and reviewed by the finance business partner. Denied expenses may be appealed once in writing to the VP of Finance, whose decision is final.

Audit
Finance audits a random 5% of submitted expenses each month, plus a full audit of all expenses for employees departing the company. Fraudulent submissions result in termination and, where applicable, recovery of funds.
